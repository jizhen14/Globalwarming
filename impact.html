GLOBALWARMING

What is the impact of global warming?

There are different impacts of global warming, but they all are having significant and costly effects on our communities, our health, and our climate. Unless we take immediate action to reduce global warming emissions, these impacts will continue to intensify, grow ever more costly and damaging, and increasingly affect the entire planet — including you, your community, and your family.

More frequent and intense heat waves



Image from: http://images.nationalgeographic.com/wpf/media-live/photos/000/001/cache/green-house-factor_177_600x450.jpg

Dangerously hot weather is already occuring more frequently than it did 60 years ago—and scientists expect heat waves to become more frequent and severe as global warming intensifies. This increase in heat waves creates serious health risks, and can lead to heat exhaustion, heat stroke, and aggravate existing medical conditions.

Source: http://www.ucsusa.org/our-work/global-warming/science-and-impacts/global-warming-impacts#.VIXsNtLF_To

Extreme weather events



Image from: http://www.earthtimes.org/newsimage/extreme-weather-motivates-greener-behaviour_213.jpg

Strong scientific evidence shows that global warming is increasing certain types of extreme weather events, including heat waves, coastal flooding, extreme precipitation events, and more severe droughts. Global warming also creates conditions that can lead to more powerful hurricanes.

Source: http://www.ucsusa.org/our-work/global-warming/science-and-impacts/global-warming-impacts#.VIXsNtLF_To
